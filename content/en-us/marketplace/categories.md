---
title: Marketplace asset categories
description: Reference of all supported Marketplace asset categories and a brief description of each.
---

<Alert severity='warning'>
Roblox is currently experimenting with automated asset categorization, which may impact how your existing and future uploads are categorized. If the system misidentifies your Marketplace asset, [report a bug](https://devforum.roblox.com/c/bug-reports/10).
</Alert>

To ensure a consistent and high-quality experience for all users, creators must categorize their uploads according to the definitions provided below. When uploading avatar assets, use the following guidelines to select your avatar asset category to the best of your ability. It's important to note that intentional [miscategorization](./marketplace-policy.md#miscategorization) is against Marketplace policy.

## Rigid accessories

See [Rigid accessories](../art/accessories/).

      <td>Face</td>
      <td>Accessories designed specifically for facial features (eyes, nose, mouth, ears). Includes items that enhance, conceal, or decorate facial areas.</td>

      <td>Neck</td>

    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Shoulder</td>

    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Front</td>

    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Back</td>

    </tr>
    <tr>

    </tr>

      <td>Body suits <br /><Typography noWrap>*(auto-detected)*</Typography></td>

    </tr>
    <tr>

## Layered accessories

See [Clothing](../art/accessories/layered-clothing.md).

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr>
      <th>Asset</th>
      <th>Description</th>
    </tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr>
      <td>T-Shirt</td>
      <td>Short-sleeved top characterized by its classic "T" silhouette and collarless neckline. It fits closely to the avatar's torso without the buttons, zippers, or formal collars.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Shirt</td>
      <td>Upper-body garments, such as dress shirts, tanktops, camisoles.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Sweater</td>
      <td>Knitted or heavy-fabric garments worn directly on the torso, typically long-sleeved, and providing moderate to significant warmth. Usually softer and more casual than jackets or coats, worn as standalone or layered garments without significant outer structure.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Pants</td>
      <td>Garments explicitly covering the waist, hips, and legs, generally extending from the waistline down to the ankles or near-ankle region, providing complete or near-complete coverage of the lower body.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Dress/Skirt</td>
      <td>Garments specifically designed to cover waist, hips, and legs without separate leg compartments. Skirts typically drape downward from the waist and can vary significantly in length, from short (above knee) to full-length (ankle or floor-length).</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Shorts</td>
      <td>Lower-body garments covering the waist, hips, and upper legs, typically ending above the knee. Provide less leg coverage compared to pants, usually informal or casual in style.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Shoes</td>
      <td>Footwear items that fully enclose the toes and front of the foot, typically designed with structured soles and upper coverage.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Body suits <br /><Typography noWrap>*(auto-detected)*</Typography></td>
      <td>A full-body layered asset that covers both the upper and lower body in a single piece.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>Full masks <br /><Typography noWrap>*(auto-detected)*</Typography></td>
      <td>An accessory or clothing designed to fully enclose or replace the character's head and face.</td>
    </tr>
  </tbody>
</table>
