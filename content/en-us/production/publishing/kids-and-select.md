Roblox is launching a unified safety and publishing framework to align content with user age. This update introduces two new age-based account types — **Roblox Kids** and **Roblox Select** —, sets new verification requirements for creators who create content for all ages, and establishes a new [evaluation process](#evaluation-process) for any game available to  users under 16.

Parents and guardians can link accounts and modify their child's access. To learn more, see [parental controls](https://about.roblox.com/parental-controls).

## Publishing requirements

To make a new experience public or update an existing public games, games depend on the target audience. For more information on publishing experiences, see [Publish experiences and places](../publishing/publish-experiences-and-places.md#publish-experiences).

### To reach 16+ and Trusted Friends

1. Complete an age check.
2. Account is in good standing and at least 2 days old.

### To reach all ages (including Kids and Select)

To reach users under 16, creators — or group owners for group-owned games — must meet a higher bar of accountability:

1. **ID Verification** — Proof of identity via a state-issued ID or a parent-linked verification. See [Account verification](./account-verification.md).
2. **Two-factor authentication** — 2FA must be enabled on your account.
3. **Publishing fee or subscription** — Satisfy one of the following:
   - Pay a one-time, refundable publishing fee of 1,000 Robux per game.
     - This fee will be refunded if your game maintains 100 highly engaged players for 60 days without moderation.
     - If the experience is permanently moderated or taken down for not meeting [Roblox Community Standards](https://en.help.roblox.com/hc/articles/203313410) within the 60 days, the fee **will not be returned**.
   - Have an active [Roblox Plus](../monetization/roblox-plus.md) or [Roblox Premium](https://www.roblox.com/premium/membership) subscription for 2 consecutive months.
4. **Evaluation** — Games must pass the [evaluation process](#evaluation-process).

<Alert severity="warning">
You only need to satisfy the publishing fee or subscription requirement when you publish a new experience or update an existing one for all ages audiences. If your subscription later expires, your experience stays published for all ages.
</Alert>

## Evaluation process

Games intended for users under 16 do not launch to those audiences immediately. New games go through the following stages:

1. **Trial phase** — The experience is first made available only to age-checked users 16 and older.
2. **Analysis of Highly Engaged users** — Engagement is analyzed through metrics like account age, play history, and spend on Roblox to verify it's from real users and not bots. Increasing engagement with age-checked 16+ users will help you progress towards becoming eligible faster.
3. **Safety review** — The experience's real-time moderation reports and gameplay are reviewed for age-suitability.
4. **Threshold** — Once the experience reaches a benchmark of 500 unique plays by highly engaged age-checked users within a 60 day window, it completes evaluation and becomes eligible for Kids and Select audiences.

To monitor your experience's evaluation progress in real time, use the **Audience Reach** dashboard in the [Creator Hub](https://create.roblox.com).

## Frequently asked questions

**Why is a publishing fee or subscription required to publish to all ages?**

Requiring either a one-time publishing fee or a recurring Roblox Plus or Premium subscription creates a meaningful financial cost for bad actors attempting to bypass publishing requirements with spam accounts, while remaining as affordable as possible for legitimate creators. Creators who are not yet ready to pay the fee or subscribe can still reach the 16+ audience.

**What if my subscription expires?**

Your experience remains published and available to all ages. You only need to satisfy the publishing fee or subscription requirement when publishing a new experience or updating an existing experience for all ages audiences.

**How do I track my experience's evaluation status?**

Use the **Audience Reach** dashboard in the [Creator Hub](https://create.roblox.com) to monitor your experience's engagement benchmarks in real time.

**What if a parent's favorite game is missing from Roblox Kids?**

Parents can use the **Approved Experience** control in parental settings to manually allow access to specific games for their child. This does not apply to Restricted (18+) content.
